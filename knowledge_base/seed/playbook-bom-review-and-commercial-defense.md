# Playbook BOM Review and Commercial Defense

- Review BOMs by architecture intent first, not by SKU list. Every row should trace back to a design assumption, resilience target, growth expectation, or operational requirement.
- Separate mandatory items from optional items and future-phase items. This prevents price shock and helps the customer understand what protects project success versus what extends capability later.
- Validate hidden commercial dependencies: support term, licensing edition, subscription duration, implementation scope, rack and power assumptions, backup targets, and network upgrades.
- A strong commercial defense explains why the BOM is structured the way it is, what risks appear if rows are removed, and which items can be safely phased.
- Before sharing totals, confirm currency assumptions, price validity window, discount conditions, and any dependencies on partner-delivered services.
