# Product UX Minimal Results Trustworthy

- The UI should be minimal and calm. Users should spend their attention on actionable results, not on dashboard decoration or complex navigation.
- Every screen should answer one practical question: what did the system understand, what does it recommend, why is it recommending that, and what can the user use immediately in a real presale motion.
- Accuracy and trust are more important than visual richness. Show the output in a form that feels like a competent presale engineer prepared it, with assumptions, risks, gaps, and recommended next actions made explicit.
- UX should make the user feel like they have their own presale resource: fast response, structured reasoning, usable recommendations, and confidence that the information can be taken into meetings, proposals, or internal reviews.
- Avoid noisy UI patterns, unnecessary controls, and decorative metrics. Prioritize clarity of recommendation, evidence, editable assumptions, and exportable results.
